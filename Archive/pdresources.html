Learning to use the tools

  • Sketch
    • The only tool you’ll basically 100% need to learn
      • Figma works too if you don’t have a Mac but I can’t speak for it
    • Crafting UIs
      • Honestly just grab an iOS or Material UI Kit and start copying and pasting and rearranging stuff onto your own separate sketch document
    • Honestly the best way to learn (after picking up the very basics) is to
      • Look at some existing files and see how people create things in sketch (layers, groups, shading, colors, borders)
      • Facebook Analytics UI Pack
      • The UI kits i linked earlier
      • Look at things you like on Dribbble and try to recreate them afterwards, great way to learn Visual UI design and using sketch
  • Origami
    • Save this for once you actually can start making UIs and flows in sketch
    • Prototyping!!!!!!!!!
    • Make things that feel just like the real app!
    • Orgami’s own tutorials are the best

Making portfolios

  • Simon Pan’s Great Portfolios are Great Stories
  • On portfolios:
    • 1st impression matters a lot. People aren’t going to spend too much time looking at your portfolio at first before making a move on/reject decision
    • Curate your projects
      • Thumbnails should look visually consistent with eachother
      • Have a wide breath of material
      • a bit of ui, a bit of ux, a bit of product, graphic, illustration, etc
      • Don’t be afraid to put other creative work on, but don’t highlight it to the point it gets in the way of the work you’re trying to get hired for
      • Not every piece deserves to be on your portfolio
    • Case Studies
      • The most important rule with these is that Case Studies are more for You than the reader
      • What I mean by this is no one will ever read it more than a quick skim, but having a good writeup provides you a wonderful framework talk about the project effectively when interviewing
      • Start by showing the final product
      • otherwise people will have to scroll to see what it looks like and no one likes doing that
      • Example Loose Template
      • Problem
      • Solution
      • Implementation
      • Don’t be afraid to talk about the Process
      • Who worked with
      • Why project was done (background)
      • Results
      • Backwards thoughts
